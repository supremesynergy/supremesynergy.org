## What to do AFTER you send (the 3-7-30 follow-up rule, Hormozi)

For anyone who reads but doesn't reply within 3 days:

**Day 3.** Soft bump: *"Hey [name], just bumping this up in case it got buried. Cohort opens Monday, no worries either way."*

**Day 7.** Value add, not pressure: *"FYI Cohort 1 is filling. Sharing this short thing I wrote about [specific concept] in case it's useful regardless of CAM: [link to anything you've written, even Notes app]"*

**Day 30.** This pushes past cohort 1, so reframe: *"Hey, cohort 1 closed full. Want me to put you on the cohort 2 list? Price will be ~$2,500 (still founding tier)."*

## Tracking sheet (Hormozi: what's measured improves)

Keep a simple list:

| Name | Channel | Sent | Replied | Status |
|------|---------|------|---------|--------|
| | DM / Email / Voxer | Date | Y/N | Hot / Warm / No / Closed |

50 DMs at 30% reply rate = 15 conversations. 15 conversations at 13% close rate = 2 closes. That fills the cohort.

## What NOT to do

- Don't open with the offer doc link as the first message. DM first, link if they ask.
- Don't mention scarcity if they're not warm. "2 seats left" reads as pressure to a cold contact.
- Don't follow up more than 3 times in a 30-day window (BamFam discipline).
- Don't lower the price in DMs. The founding rate IS the discount; protect the floor.
- Don't pitch in NS group chats or Skool posts. Feels promotional, breaks the warm-network trust.
